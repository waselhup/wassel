# A11 — Content is professional, never trend-bait

**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Status:** Accepted
**Rule:** R11

## Context

Generic LinkedIn-post AI tools produce hook-driven, trend-chasing copy ("Stop scrolling — here's the ONE thing every PM should do today!"). This tone is wrong for senior GCC professionals.

## Decision

The Career Content pillar (Sprint 5) generates posts and carousels in a calm professional register, drawing on the user's actual experience surfaced in `career_profile` and recent activity. The prompt explicitly forbids:
- Hook patterns ("Stop scrolling…", "I cannot believe this…")
- Vision 2030 references (banned globally)
- Fabricated statistics
- Hashtag spam (max 3 hashtags per post)

The prompt explicitly encourages:
- A concrete observation from the user's experience
- One actionable insight
- A modest closing invitation ("ما تجربتك؟" / "What's your experience?")

## Consequences

- `docs/prompts/content-post.md` carries the tone constraints
- Sprint 5 includes copy QA against a small set of golden examples
- Marketing posts about content generation use restrained examples

## Alternatives Rejected

- Match popular LinkedIn engagement-bait patterns — rejected, off-brand
- Make tone a Settings toggle — rejected, dilutes the brand
