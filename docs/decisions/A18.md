# A18 — Subscription tokens expire; top-up tokens don't

**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Status:** Accepted
**Rule:** R18

## Context

Mixed-source wallets without expiration rules either reward hoarding (everything is lifetime) or punish active users (everything expires). Neither matches the price-anchor reality: subscriptions are *commitments*; top-ups are *purchases*.

## Decision

The 3-wallet system enforces:

| Wallet | Source | TTL | Consumption order |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `wallet_bonus` | Promos, refunds, Explore plan | 90 days (configurable) | 1st |
| `wallet_subscription` | Monthly plan renewal | Until end of month | 2nd |
| `wallet_topup` | Paid top-ups | None (lifetime) | 3rd |

The pricing page, the wallet display, and the post-purchase confirmation all explain this in plain language. Subscription tokens that don't get used by month end are forfeited (not refunded, not rolled over).

## Consequences

- The 3-wallet migration (Sprint 1) creates the three tables and the v2 RPC
- The wallet display in the UI shows three balances + their expiry
- Monthly Supabase cron resets `wallet_subscription` on the user's renewal date

## Alternatives Rejected

- Single wallet with no expiration — rejected, encourages hoarding, makes monthly economics noisy
- Single wallet, all expires — rejected, kills top-up purchase incentive
